A Smarter Way
to Publish

How Swifts Coalition can go from Discord conversations to published articles — without the chaos.

The Big Picture

What We're Building

Right now, getting an article from a writer to the website involves a lot of back and forth. Someone writes something, sends it over, someone else formats it, uploads it, finds the images, assigns the author, hits publish. It works, but it's slow and things fall through the cracks.

What we're proposing is a system that lives inside our Discord server and does most of the heavy lifting automatically. A writer fills out a simple form right inside Discord. A bot reads it, checks that everything is in order, and sends it to the website as a draft. Staff review it, and when they're happy, they hit publish. That's it.

"Writers write. Editors edit. The bot handles everything in between."

No one needs to touch the WordPress dashboard unless they want to. No more copy-pasting articles. No more hunting for images. No more forgetting to set the author.

How Writers Submit

The Template

Every article starts with a template. Think of it like a form, but it lives in Discord and you just fill it out in plain text. A writer pastes it into the submissions channel, fills in their details, and posts it along with their images.

Here's what a filled-out template looks like:

TITLE: City Council Votes to Cut Parks Budget AUTHOR: Jamie Rivera CATEGORY: Local Government TAGS: parks, budget, city council FEATURED_IMAGE: council-chambers.jpg --- The vote came just after midnight on Tuesday, with five council members in favor and two opposed... [IMAGE: protest-outside.jpg | Residents protest outside City Hall | alt: A crowd holding signs outside City Hall at night] The proposed cuts would affect twelve parks across the district... [IMAGE: parks-map.jpg | Map showing affected parks | alt: A city map with twelve parks highlighted in red] Council member Diaz said the decision sets a troubling precedent.

The writer attaches their image files to the same Discord message. The bot renames them automatically, shows the writer the corrected names, and hands back an updated template ready to resubmit.

Who Does What

Three Tiers, Clear Lines

The system has three types of people, each with a defined role. Nobody can do something they're not supposed to.

Contributors

  • Submit articles using the template
  • Attach images to their submission
  • Resubmit if something gets flagged
  • Cannot publish or pull drafts
  • Cannot edit others' work directly

Editors & Staff

  • Review drafts before they go live
  • Pull any article for editing
  • Hand corrected templates to writers
  • Override author assignments
  • Approve and publish with one click

Writers write. Editors decide what's ready. The system enforces it so nobody has to police it themselves.

Author Pages

Real Author Pages. No Accounts Required.

Every article will show a proper author with a bio page and a list of everything they've written. Authors don't need to sign up for WordPress. They don't need a password. They don't need to touch the website at all.

The first time a writer's name appears in a template, the system creates their author profile automatically and notifies staff so someone can add a bio and photo when ready. After that, every article they submit links to that profile automatically.

If a name is misspelled or left out, a staff member can override it before the article goes live.

Images

Images That Go Exactly Where You Put Them

Writers place a simple tag in the text wherever they want an image to appear. The bot reads those tags, takes the uploaded files, and places them exactly where indicated on the website — captions and alt text included.

If a template references an image that wasn't uploaded, the whole submission gets flagged and nothing goes to the website. The bot tells the writer exactly what's missing. If no featured image is declared, the bot waits two minutes then defaults to the first image and notes this in the review channel so staff are aware.

Making Changes

Editing Is Simple. On Purpose.

Contributors cannot edit drafts on their own. If something needs to change, the process goes through staff. A staff member pastes the article's link into Discord. The bot fetches that article and hands back a fully filled-out template ready to copy. The staff member passes it to the writer, who edits it and submits it like a brand new article.

The link is embedded in the template itself, so the bot knows to update the existing draft rather than create a new one. Staff review the updated version before anything changes on the live site.
